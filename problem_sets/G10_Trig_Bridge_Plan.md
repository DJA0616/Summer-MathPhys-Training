## Easy (8 questions, 24 pts)

### Q1. [MC] In a right triangle, sin(θ) = opposite/hypotenuse. If the side opposite angle θ is 3 cm and the hypotenuse is 5 cm, what is sin(θ)?
- A) 3/5
- B) 4/5
- C) 5/3
- D) 5/4
- Correct: A
- Difficulty: easy

### Q2. [MC] What is cos(60°)?

- B) 1/2
- C) √2/2
- D) 0
- Correct: B
- Difficulty: easy

### Q3. [INT] A ladder leans against a wall at a 60° angle. If the ladder is 10 m long, how far is the base from the wall? (Use cos(60°) = 0.5)
- Answer: 5
- Difficulty: easy

### Q4. [TF] tan(45°) = 1

- D) 1
- Correct: A
- Difficulty: easy

### Q6. [INT] In a 30-60-90 triangle, if the shortest side is 4 cm, what is the length of the hypotenuse?
- Answer: 8
- Difficulty: easy

### Q7. [TF] sin(90°) = 0
- Correct: false

### Q8. [MC] What is tan(30°)?
- A) √3/3
- B) 1/√3
- C) √3
- D) 3
- Correct: A
- Explain: tan(30°) = sin(30°)/cos(30°) = (1/2)/(√3/2) = 1/√3 = √3/3. Both A and B are equivalent — A is the rationalized form.
- Difficulty: easy

## Medium (8 questions, 32 pts)

### Q9. [MC] A flagpole casts a shadow 12 m long when the sun's angle of elevation is 30°. How tall is the flagpole? (Use tan(30°) ≈ 0.577)
- A) 6 m
- B) 7 m
- C) 8 m
- D) 9 m
- Correct: B
- Explain: tan(30°) = height / shadow → height = 12 × tan(30°) = 12 × 0.577 ≈ 6.92 m ≈ 7 m.
- Difficulty: medium

### Q10. [INT] A ramp rises 2 m over a horizontal distance of 2√3 m. What is the angle of elevation in degrees?
- Answer: 30
- Hint: tan(θ) = rise / run = 2 / (2√3) = 1/√3. What angle has this tangent?
- Difficulty: medium

### Q11. [TF] sin(θ) = cos(90° − θ) for any angle θ
- Correct: true
- Difficulty: medium

### Q12. [MC] If sin(θ) = 0.6 and θ is in the first quadrant, what is cos(θ)?
- A) 0.4
- B) 0.6
- C) 0.8
- D) 1.0
- Correct: C
- Explain: cos(θ) = √(1 − sin²θ) = √(1 − 0.36) = √0.64 = 0.8.
- Difficulty: medium

### Q13. [INT] A right triangle has legs of length 5 cm and 12 cm. What is the length of the hypotenuse?
- Answer: 13
- Difficulty: medium

### Q14. [MC] What is the value of sin²(45°) + cos²(45°)?
- A) 0
- B) 0.5
- C) 1
- D) 2
- Correct: C
- Explain: sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1 for any angle θ — the fundamental Pythagorean identity.
- Difficulty: medium

### Q15. [TF] tan(θ) = sin(θ)/cos(θ) is undefined when cos(θ) = 0
- Correct: true
- Difficulty: medium

### Q16. [INT] In right triangle ABC, angle A = 30°, angle B = 60°, and side AB = 10 cm (hypotenuse). What is the length of side BC (opposite angle A)?
- Answer: 5
- Hint: Side opposite 30° = hypotenuse × sin(30°) = 10 × 0.5.
- Difficulty: medium

## Hard (4 questions, 20 pts)

### Q17. [MC] From the top of a 50 m building, the angle of depression to a car on the ground is 30°. How far is the car from the base of the building?
- A) 28.9 m
- B) 50.0 m
- C) 86.6 m
- D) 100.0 m
- Correct: C
- Explain: Angle of depression = angle of elevation from car. tan(30°) = 50/d → d = 50/tan(30°) = 50/(1/√3) = 50√3 ≈ 86.6 m.
- Difficulty: hard

### Q18. [INT] A kite is flying at a height of 30 m. If the string makes a 60° angle with the ground, what is the length of the string to the nearest integer?
- Answer: 35
- Hint: height = length × sin(60°). So length = height / sin(60°) = 30 / (√3/2) = 60/√3 ≈ 34.64 m.
- Difficulty: hard

### Q19. [TF] For any angle θ, sin(θ + 90°) = cos(θ)
- Correct: true
- Difficulty: hard

### Q20. [MC] If cos(θ) = 0.8 and θ is in the fourth quadrant, what is sin(θ)?
- A) −0.6
- B) 0.6
- C) −0.8
- D) 0.8
- Correct: A
- Explain: sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1 → sin²(θ) = 1 − 0.64 = 0.36 → sin(θ) = ±0.6. In QIV, sine is negative → −0.6.
- Difficulty: hard
